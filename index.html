About Foundation Year

Computer science (CS) with an Integrated Foundation Year is a one-year program to help develop your skills and understanding of specific courses to make sure you can progress to study your first year in the computer science department with ease. Students in this course have to take 12 different courses, which could include mathematics, physics, chemistry, Academic skills, ICT, and a project related to CS. Studying the foundation year will involve different study approaches, like attending lectures, small group tutorials, seminars, and working on a project. Evaluation throughout the year is detained using coursework, final examination, and presentations. Lastly, one notable feature is getting high academic and pastoral support from the university advisors and support team.
