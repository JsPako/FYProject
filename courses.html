Facilities

Engineering Building

One of the largest higher education projects in the UK is the renovation of the Manchester Engineering Campus Development (MECD). MECD has built a world-class teaching, learning, and research laboratory to nurture tomorrow's engineers, scientists, and innovators. In Engineering Buildings A and B you'll have instant access to various lecture halls, bookable conference and teaching rooms, and a large selection of informal study places with both PCs and touchdown spots for your own device. Hundreds of modular teaching and learning rooms may be found inside Engineering Buildings A and B, whether you're interested in aerospace or artificial intelligence, or tackling global warming.

Sackville Building

The Sackville Building is a university building in Central Manchester. Also located near Picadilly train staion opposite The Renold Building. It is Situated on Sackville Street. It was officially opened in 1902 by Arthur Balfour, the Prime Minister at the time. This building consists of 12 floors. This building hosts a variety of rooms from classrooms to lecture theatres. The University uses this building for a variety of services and departments. These include administration, science and technology instruction and research, and examinations. The Sackville Street Building houses the University of Manchester's Electrical and Electronic Engineering (EEE) department, as well as the Dalton Institute, which houses textile sciences and nuclear research.

Renold Building

The Renold Building is a university building in Central Manchester. Located near Picadilly train staion. It was opened on 23 November 1962 and named after Sir Charles Renold (1883-1967). This building hosts 8 floors with a number of lecture theatres of varying sizes, these include five 140-seat theatres, two 300-seat theatres, a 500-seat theatre, as well as this there are a number of classrooms, Tutorials often occur here as class sizes can be quite small.

Alan Gilbert

The Alan Gilbert Learning Commons is a studying and learning facility located in the heart of the Oxford Road campus. It has an onsite café, an outstanding atrium with WiFi access, and a variety of flexible study rooms and surroundings. There are 30 bookable Group Study Rooms throughout the building varying in size to take up to 12 people maximum. There are also 24 open plan group study areas (not bookable) each with a PC and media screen. One thing that distinguishes Alan Gilbert building is that it never closes, open 24 hours all week long. Lastly, the Café, located in the lobby, offers a broad variety of hot and cold foods and beverages in a friendly and relaxed setting.

Main library

The university's main library is located on the Oxford Road campus, with the Alan Gilbert Learning Commons just in front of it. The library offers a variety of services and materials to its users, including a comprehensive collection of electronic resources. There are 14 group study rooms with a PC and a 32-inch LCD display that can accommodate up to eight individuals in a scheduled session. The main library is also known to be one of the University's key computer cluster locations. Lastly, vending machines are accessible throughout the day to sell food and drinks.
